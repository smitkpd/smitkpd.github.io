Learning CNC Machining

(Aug 2022 - Feb 2023)

Titan 2M

I machined this part on a Haas 2-Axis CNC Mill, as an introduction to CNC machining. It is a semi-complex part, containining slot features and holes. I learned to program the 2D profile toolpaths and hole drilling in Fusion 360 CAM (Computer Aided Machining). By prearing tools, workholding, securing stock in the machine and running the machine (under supervision), I gained an understanding of how tool clearances and external part profile/features affect ease-of-manufacturability and machine time.

Titan 9M

This is a more complex learning part, with 3D curved profiles and counterbore operations. First, I used a flat end mill to remove material and create steps for the 3D curved surface, then I used a ball end mill to get a smooth surface from the steps. Further, I learned how to efficiently select contour paths to reduce machine time, thus cost. I was quite happy with the final result!

Closing thoughts

By creating CAM and machining these parts, I learned how to adjust toolpaths and tool entry points to reduce machine time, as well as optimize stock dimensions. I also got a sense of making design considerations (depth of slots, fillets, chamfers, etc.) for ease of manufacturability.
