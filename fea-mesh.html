Purdue Space Program: Upper Strut FEA

(Jan - July 2022)

Bolt hole stresses

For the FEM of upper airframe struts, I improved the mesh and bolt contact modeling, as well as conducted a mesh convergence study. Sharp transitions of tetrahedral mesh elements at the bolt holes led to unrealistically high stresses, giving a Safety Margin less than zero. To resolve this, I refined the mesh around bolt holes using a Sphere of Influence control and non-linear material properties. Additionally, I experimented with different bolt diameters and strut flange thicknesses to reduce stress concentrations. Lastly, I validated stress in vicinity of bolt holes with hand calculations.

Mesh convergence

To ensure FEA results were not biased on mesh density, I conducted a mesh convergence study. The plots show how Max Von Mises and bolt hole stresses converge to within +/- 500 psi. Although, the Max Von Mises stress (40 kpsi) was above Yield Stress of Alumnium, we reasoned this was likely a FEA artifact due to distribution of nodes at the bolt hole rim. From this process, I learned how to manage computational expense efficiently, by having finer mesh in areas of high stress concentrations, and relatively coarser mesh in other areas.
