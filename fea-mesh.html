Purdue Space Program: Upper Strut FEA

(Jan - July 2022)

Bolt hole stresses

For the FEM of upper airframe struts, I improved the mesh and bolt contact modeling, as well as conducted a mesh convergence study. Sharp transitions of tetrahedral mesh elements at the bolt holes led to unrealistically high stresses, giving a Safety Margin less than zero. To resolve this, I refined the mesh around bolt holes using a Sphere of Influence control and non-linear material properties. Additionally, I experimented with different bolt diameters and strut flange thicknesses to reduce stress concentrations. Lastly, I validated stress in vicinity of bolt holes with hand calculations.

Mesh convergence

To ensure FEA results were not biased on mesh density, I conducted a mesh convergence study. The plots show how max Von Mises and bolt hole stresses converge to within +/- 500 psi. Having the stress error smaller, i.e. a finer mesh increased the computational expense quite a lot. For this reason, my team decided to allow for very little yielding for these Aluminum struts, with ~ 40 kpsi stress concentration at bolt hole rim. From this, I learned how to manage computational expense efficiently, by having finer mesh in areas of high stress concentrations, and relatively coarser mesh in other areas.
