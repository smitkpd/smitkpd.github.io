Fin Flutter Analysis

(Jan - July 2022)

During initial design of my rocket team's fins, I investigated flutter to prevent structural failure due to resonance (external vibrations correspond with structure's natural frequency) during flight. I started by referencing NACA TN 4197 and NACA Report 685, to understand underlying factors which cause flutter. I wanted to design a sandwich composite structure (to reduce mass), hence the analysis was more challenging compared to that for a isotropic material (e.g: Aluminum).

Following was my preliminary approach:-

  • • Create FEM (Finite Element Model) of the composite fin in Ansys Composite PrepPost (ACP)

  • • Obtain natural frequencies and mode shapes through FE Modal analysis

  • • Adjust natural frequencies through stiffness changes (by varying carbon fiber ply count and orientation) using the Strouhal number correlation

After consulting more experienced team members and engineers in industry, I learned the following:-

  • • Simplifying analysis by using a representative cantilever beam model for the fin (with similar material properties) and validating fin's stiffness from computational model with a Force-Deflection test to characterize response to stiffness changes.

  • • A Modal analysis on its own wouldn't be useful, as the rocket's PSD (Power Spectral Density) curve would be needed to target resonant frequencies with greater strain energy for avoiding structural failure.

Although, I ended up changing to aluminum fins in the detailed design phase (due to greater mass savings in other parts of lower airframe), I gained a sense of how to simplify the flutter problem to not fall into the trap of analysis paralysis.
