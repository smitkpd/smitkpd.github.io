Subsonic Wind Tunnel

(May - July 2023)

Introduction

I have always wanted to make a subsonic wind tunnel due to two main reasons: (1) Visualize cool streamlines over various types of airfoils and (2) Measure lift and drag force over test articles and compare with NACA charts / CFD simulations.

Design

Minimizing build cost and ensuring ease of transportation were the two main design constraints. I started by choosing the inlet's contraction ratio, as this would drive the size of the test section and diffuser. I chose a 5:1 contraction ratio for maximum airspeed inside the test section, with inlet dimensions constrained by cheaper stock.

Inlet: Originally I designed a cone-shaped inlet, but then changed to a polynomial-curve shape for better flow characteristics. I cut out a 5th degree polynomial shape out of cardboard sheets using a laser cutter, and plan to epoxy them together. Hinges will be used to remove the inlet and install test articles in the test section. To ensure laminar airflow, I plan to use 2-3 layers of window mesh to break down turbulent eddies in the intake air.

Test section: A sting arm protruding inside the test section will hold the test article. Load cells will be connected to the sting arm for measuring lift and drag forces.

Diffuser: By increasing the area, the diffuser recovers pressure losses from high-speed flow in the test section, and prevents backflow of air. It also contains the fan at the aft end, which pulls the intake air into the wind tunnel.

Electronics: I will be using two load cells for lift and drag measurements, by connecting them to an Arduino. I calibrated the load cells with known weights.

Manufacturing

The inlet, test section and diffuser section are made from 0.5” thick plywood. I used a CNC Gantry router (at Purdue’s Bechtel Innovation Design Center ) to cut out the sections. Then, I assembled them using two part structural epoxy and wood screws. I plan to complete manufacturing and start initial wind tunnel tests, by end of December 2023 (after my Fall internship).
