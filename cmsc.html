Heating Coil Experiment

(May 2024 - Present)

At the Composites Manufacturing and Simulation Center (CMSC), I am designing, analyzing and conducting experiments to characterize temperature of 3D printed filament based on a heating coil embedded between filament layers. The objective is to provide localized heating in different regions of the tooling for curing a composite part with varying thickness and thermal conductivity.

Specifically, I am evaluating how various stretch factors of the heating coil affect the filament's temperature. I determined relations between coil resistance and applied strain, using Castigliano's theorem for spring deflection and resistance/inch from the coil's datasheet. Additionally, I wrote a Python script to plot coil temperature as a function of applied strain using 1D, steady-state heat transfer and surface energy balance.

To validate my hand calculations, I conducted an experiment where I passed current through the coil and measured its temperature for various strains, using a thermal camera. Next, I made a Python script to analyze thermal images and determine the coil's median temperature. Fig. 4 shows that the experimental data aligned well with the prediction, except a few outliers. One challenge was the lack of control on the lab's ventilation system, which could have changed the convection coefficient, thus coil temperature. To resolve this, I covered the coil under a cardboard box, however, it had an opening for the thermal camera, allowing the surrounding air currents to affect the coil's temperature and lead to outliers in Fig. 4.
