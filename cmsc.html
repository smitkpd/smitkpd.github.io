Heating Coil Experiment

(May 2024 - Present)

At the Composites Manufacturing and Simulation Center (CMSC), I am working on embedding heating coils between layers of 3D printed tooling (for composite parts) to provide localized heating in different regions of the tooling. This is required for curing a part with varying thickness and thermal conductivity. Specifically, I am responsible for characterizing filament temperature based on how much a heating coil is stretched. The following describes my approach to this problem:-

  • • The filament temperature is proportional to the power dissipated by the coil -> related to coil's resistance -> proportional to the equivalent straight length of the coil. To account for springback in the coil when stretched, I related the applied strain to plastic strain through hand calculations for coil deflection, based on Castigliano's theorem. Next, I computed the resistance for a reference section, based on its new pitch and straight wire length. To validate my calculations, I experimentally measured the final coil length after stretching it to a certain length. Additionally, I measured resistance using a multimeter. Fig. 2 shows that the experimental results followed the prediction quite well.

  • • Currently, I am validating the prediction for the coil's surface temperature with experimental results. I am computing the coil temperature using a 1D, steady-state heat transfer analysis, with convection and radiation. To measure the coil's temperature, I connected the coil to a variable power supply, and measured temperature using a thermal camera. I used Python to determine a median temperature, to avoid bias from hotspots in the coil.
